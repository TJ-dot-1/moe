The Textile Waste System Is Broken

Every year, millions of tonnes of textiles are discarded globally—yet most are still wearable or recyclable. In Europe alone, the majority of post-consumer textiles are either incinerated or landfilled, despite clear opportunities for reuse and recycling.

At the same time:

Growing Demand

Demand for affordable clothing continues to grow unprecedentedly.

Underdeveloped Systems

Waste management systems remain underdeveloped in emerging markets.

Lost Resources

Valuable materials are lost instead of reused.

The result is a system that wastes resources, increases emissions, and misses economic opportunities.

A Smarter Circular Textile System

Moenviron is building an integrated approach to textile circularity—starting with what works today and scaling toward what’s needed tomorrow. Our model focuses on three key pillars:

01

Textile Recovery & Sourcing

02

Reuse & Redistribution

By aligning with existing second-hand markets, we extend the life of garments and unlock immediate economic value.

03

Circular Processing (Next Phase)

Why Africa Matters in Circular Textiles

Africa plays a critical role in the global textile circular economy.

In markets like Kenya:

  • Second-hand clothing is a primary source of affordable fashion
  • Demand drives strict quality selection
  • Entire ecosystems of traders, retailers, and consumers depend on this system

Far from being waste destinations, these markets are active value creators—extending the life of textiles and generating economic opportunity.

Moenviron is positioning itself within this ecosystem to build more structured, transparent, and scalable circular solutions.
